<h1 class="part-title">Part V</h1>
<h1 class="part-subtitle">The 2026 Frontier</h1>

<div class="callout">
<p><strong>Where the cutting edge actually cuts:</strong> Everything in Parts I through IV will remain useful for years. The fundamentals of software, the mechanics of LLMs, the patterns of AI product engineering, the discipline of production systems — these are durable. This part is different. This part covers what is happening <em>right now</em>, at the boundary between what works and what might work, between what's proven and what's promising. Some of what you read here will age beautifully. Some will be obsolete before the ink dries. That's the nature of a frontier — and learning to navigate that uncertainty is itself a critical builder skill.</p>
<p><strong>What you'll understand after this part:</strong> Why the future of AI isn't bigger models but smarter combinations of models. How reasoning models that "think before answering" are changing what's possible — and what they cost. Why voice is the interface that unlocks the next billion AI users, especially in India. How AI systems that improve themselves are moving from research curiosity to production reality. And what the next two to three years likely hold for builders who are paying attention.</p>
<p><strong>What you'll build:</strong> Not code in this part — but judgment. The ability to evaluate a new model announcement and separate signal from hype in under five minutes. The instinct to know when a frontier technique is ready for production and when it needs another six months of baking. A mental model for where AI is headed that lets you make architectural bets today that won't need to be ripped out tomorrow. This is the part that turns you from someone who builds with AI into someone who sees where AI building is going.</p>
<p><strong>The shift this part demands:</strong> Parts I through IV gave you certainty — proven patterns, battle-tested approaches, established best practices. Part V asks you to get comfortable with uncertainty. The compound AI system you design today might be obsolete when a single model can do it all next year. The reasoning model you're paying premium prices for today might be commodity-priced in six months. The frontier rewards builders who hold their architectures loosely, swap components without sentiment, and treat every technical choice as a hypothesis rather than a commitment.</p>
</div>
